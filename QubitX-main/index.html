Quantum Computing Fundamentals

What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computing is a revolutionary technology that uses quantum mechanical phenomena like superposition and entanglement to process information in ways that classical computers cannot. Unlike classical bits that are either 0 or 1, quantum bits (qubits) can exist in multiple states simultaneously.

Harnesses quantum mechanics for computation
Exponentially faster for certain problems
Fundamentally different from classical computing

Classical Bits vs Qubits

Classical Bits

  • Can only be 0 or 1
  • Deterministic measurement
  • No superposition
  • Limited information capacity
0
or
1

Qubits

  • Can be |0⟩, |1⟩, or superposition
  • Probabilistic measurement
  • Infinite possible states
  • Exponential information capacity
|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩
|α|² + |β|² = 1

Superposition and Measurement

Superposition allows qubits to exist in multiple states simultaneously until measured. This is like a coin spinning in the air - it's both heads and tails until it lands.

Before Measurement:
|0⟩ + |1⟩
Measurement
After Measurement:
|0⟩ or |1⟩

Dirac Notation and Quantum States

Dirac notation (bra-ket notation) is the mathematical language of quantum mechanics, providing a clean way to represent quantum states and operations.

|ψ⟩ A quantum state (ket)
⟨φ| The complex conjugate (bra)
⟨φ|ψ⟩ Inner product (bracket)
|0⟩, |1⟩ Computational basis states

Measurement Probabilities

What are Measurement Probabilities?

P(|0⟩) = |α|² - Probability of measuring the qubit in state |0⟩

P(|1⟩) = |β|² - Probability of measuring the qubit in state |1⟩

These probabilities are derived from the squared magnitudes of the complex amplitudes α and β.

Note: P(|0⟩) + P(|1⟩) = 1 (normalization)

P(|0⟩): 1.000 (100.0%)
P(|1⟩): 0.000 (0.0%)

Bloch Sphere Representation

What is the Bloch Sphere?

A 3D geometric representation of qubit states where:

  • North Pole (Z+): |0⟩ state
  • South Pole (Z-): |1⟩ state
  • X-axis: |+⟩ and |-⟩ states
  • Y-axis: |i+⟩ and |i-⟩ states

Coordinates: Any point (θ, φ) represents a valid qubit state

θ = polar angle (0 to π), φ = azimuthal angle (0 to 2π)

Spherical Coordinates
θ (polar): 0.000 rad (0.0°)
φ (azimuthal): 0.000 rad (0.0°)

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: State Creation

Create a qubit state with:

  • 75% chance of measuring |0⟩
  • 25% chance of measuring |1⟩

Solution: α = √0.75 ≈ 0.866, β = √0.25 = 0.5

θ ≈ 1.047 radians (60°), φ = 0

Exercise 2: Gate Sequences

Starting from |0⟩, apply the sequence:

  • Hadamard → X → Hadamard

Solution: |0⟩ → |+⟩ → |-⟩ → |1⟩

Final state: |1⟩

Exercise 3: Inner Products

Calculate the inner product:

  • ⟨+|0⟩ = ?

Solution: ⟨+|0⟩ = 1/√2 ≈ 0.707

This represents the probability amplitude of measuring |+⟩ when the system is in |0⟩
